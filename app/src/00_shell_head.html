How to read this app, in plain language

The short version of every concept the numbers rely on. Each figure in the app also carries its own i button with the specifics, and every site scorecard can trace a number back to its data source.
Expected annual cost (AAL)The average yearly bill if this year could be rerun thousands of times: most runs cost little, rare disaster runs cost a lot, and this is the long-run average. It is the number insurers price from. Return period"1-in-100" means a 1% chance each year, not an event that arrives on schedule. Two can land in one decade; that is chance behaving normally, not a broken model. Value at RiskThe size of a bad year at a stated rarity: what a 1-in-100 year would cost the whole portfolio, damage plus lost operating profit. Risk bandsPlain-language grades (Minimal to Severe) cut from expected loss as a share of value, so sites can be sorted and discussed without decimals. Pathways and horizonsSSP pathways are IPCC emissions futures (SSP1-2.6 low, SSP2-4.5 middle, SSP5-8.5 high) at 2030, 2050, and 2080. They are what-ifs to compare, not forecasts to believe. Benefit-cost ratio (BCR)Averted losses over the appraisal horizon, brought to today's dollars, divided by the upfront cost. Above 1.0, the measure returns more than it costs in avoided loss alone.
Technical premiumThe expected loss an insurance layer takes on, times a loading for the insurer's capital and expenses. A benchmark for judging quotes, not a market price. Risk toleranceYour stated line between monitor and act, set on the Summary tab. The app flags whatever crosses it; where the line sits is a business decision the app never makes for you. Uncertainty bandNo single number here is exact. The low-to-high range says how far the truth could plausibly sit from the central estimate, and the tornado chart names the largest single reason. Where the data comes fromPublic climate science, run through the CLIMADA pipeline into a hazard grid this app reads. The per-peril chips on this tab show green only when that grid is live, covers every scenario, and reached every loaded site; a site outside a peril's coverage is shown degraded on that peril (a clearly labeled interim approximation fills in, for exploration only), never a silent zero. What this app never doesFill a data gap silently. Perils without data score zero and say why, interim figures are labeled everywhere they appear, and nothing you load ever leaves your browser.

Known interim limits. Coastal-flood depth is keyed to open-coast proximity, so sheltered below-sea-level basins (for example New Orleans behind levees) are understated, and the latitude-based heat proxy underrepresents arid inland microclimates. These resolve when a CLIMADA hazard grid is loaded. Measure costs and strengths are planning-grade defaults drawn from published mitigation studies; replace them with engineering estimates before committing capital. Treat figures as screening estimates, not disclosure, until then.

The broker evidence pack (Adaptation tab, risk-layering panel) writes one row per site: verified secondary modifiers (roof type and year, opening protection, first-floor elevation, elevated equipment, defenses, wildfire attributes) plus present-day modeled direct EAD, the top peril, the 1-in-100 loss, and a hazard_source column stating whether figures came from a CLIMADA grid or the interim model. The action list (Adaptation tab, action queue) writes every ranked site-and-measure pair with averted loss, cost, BCR, funded status, and the appraisal assumptions (scenario, horizon, discount rate); when a results pack is loaded, its canonical capital-plan rows are appended and labeled climada_pack. The owner column ships blank on purpose.

Export artifacts & schemas (v3)

Every surface exports the exact data behind what it shows, scoped to the selected scenario and labeled with it. These files feed the SharePoint-to-Power-BI pipeline, so the formats are stable by contract: new artifacts get new files; within a file, columns only ever append at the tail, never rename or reorder. The legacy Power BI export above is a frozen contract and is unchanged.
Provenance vocabularyPer-field provenance columns use four values, never blended: sov (a fact on file from the SOV or profile), calibrated (modeled AND grounded by the loaded loss run within the 0.5x-2x band), modeled (event-set or pack math), estimated (interim model, default terms, or any fallback). Welcome

What this tool does, in one minute

It puts a dollar figure on the climate risk facing the resort portfolio: how much wind, flood, extreme heat, wildfire and storm rainfall are expected to cost each site every year, today and as the climate warms toward 2080. Then it helps you decide what to do about it: harden the buildings, insure, or accept the risk.

To get going:

  • Load the sample portfolio from Portfolio in the top bar to explore with illustrative resorts, or
  • Search a place in the top bar to add a site, or upload your own list via Portfolio.

Every figure has a small i button that explains it in plain language. Nothing you load ever leaves your browser.
