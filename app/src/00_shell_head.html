Hazard source

The app runs on one of two hazard sources. This is the current one.
Load CLIMADA hazard grid + provenance
Drop hazard_grid.csv, hazard_grid_meta.json, and results_pack.json from the pipeline, together or one at a time, or click to browse. Grid schema: lat, lon, scenario, hazard, v10..v500
Until a grid is loaded, hazard comes from built-in interim models where an honest one exists (wildfire needs the site's wui_class; TC rainfall has no interim model at all and stays zero without a grid). Good for exploration, not for disclosure. A CLIMADA grid supersedes any peril it carries.

Known interim limits. Coastal-flood depth is keyed to open-coast proximity, so sheltered below-sea-level basins (for example New Orleans behind levees) are understated, and the latitude-based heat proxy underrepresents arid inland microclimates. These resolve when a CLIMADA hazard grid is loaded. Measure costs and strengths are planning-grade defaults drawn from published mitigation studies; replace them with engineering estimates before committing capital. Treat figures as screening estimates, not disclosure, until then.
Wiring to Power BI: the exported CSV carries per-site EAD and rating for every peril, combined physical EAD and %, heat indicators, combined return-period losses, and the full financial layer (per-site revenue, direct damage, business interruption, heat revenue at risk, and total climate AAL). Run refresh_hazard.py on a schedule to keep the multi-peril hazard grid current (add a hazard column per row), load it here, and the same schema feeds your RtV model and the 17-resort backtest.
